Background

About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Mechanical & Electrical Engineering Department at Wilkes University, Wilkes-Barre, PA. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Missouri S&T in 2025, advised by Prof. Sarangapani Jagannathan.

My research focuses on designing intelligent, safe, and adaptive control systems for uncertain nonlinear and cyber-physical systems — spanning adaptive optimal control, deep continual reinforcement learning, neuromorphic/spiking neural networks, and human–robot teaming. During my Ph.D., my work was funded by the Army Research Office (ARO), Office of Naval Research (ONR), and Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR). I am currently PI on pending NSF proposals in graph neural control and spiking neural networks.

I received my M.Tech. in Cyber-Physical Systems from IIT Jodhpur, India, working on integral sliding mode control for microgrid ripple mitigation funded by DST India, advised by Dr. Deepak Fulwani.

🌟 Summer Mentoring Grant — $9,559 (Funded, 2026)
Student Research Mentorship Program, Wilkes University

We develop and apply adaptive control algorithms on TurtleBot 4 and TurtleBot 4 Lite robotic platforms. Students implement and test advanced trajectory tracking algorithms in real-time, including optimal control strategies derived from actor-critic reinforcement learning frameworks. The robots navigate complex environments while maintaining safety constraints enforced by barrier Lyapunov functions.

Key activities include: trajectory tracking of reference paths in 2D environments, formation control with multi-robot coordination, obstacle avoidance using real-time sensor feedback, hardware-in-the-loop validation of theoretical control laws, and integration of ROS2 for real-time execution. Students gain hands-on experience bridging theory and practice in adaptive nonlinear control systems.

Neuromorphic Computing
Adaptive Spiking Neural Control

Safety-critical control using spiking neural networks (SNNs) — biologically inspired, event-driven architectures achieving 80% energy savings over conventional ANNs. The framework embeds safety via Augmented Barrier States (ABS), uses SVD-regularized online weight update laws to handle spike discontinuities, and achieves uniformly ultimately bounded (UUB) tracking. A second thrust applies SNN-based intent observers for human–multi-robot collaboration.

LIVE SIMULATION — LIF neuron membrane · spike encoding · SNN layers · closed-loop control · online weight adaptation

Human–Robot Teaming
Human–Robot Interaction & Teaming

Two-level adaptive control for safe cooperative human–robot manipulation. High-level: admittance model M̂ẍ+B̂ẋ+K̂x=F_h with online intent NN estimating {M̂,B̂,K̂} from human force. Low-level: distributed actor-critic RL (one per agent) solving coupled HJB equations with neighbor info. Safety via Barrier Lyapunov Functions + KKT conditions. All weights (critic, actor, intent NN) updated online simultaneously.

LIVE SIMULATION — admittance intent NN · actor-critic RL per agent · BLF/KKT safety · two-level closed-loop · weight convergence

Multi-Agent Systems
Adaptive Graph Neural Control

New foundations connecting non-stationary RL, Lyapunov-based adaptive optimal control, and GNN function approximation. Shared weights W^(ℓ) across all agents at each layer. Message passing m_ji=φ_m(h_j,h_i,e_ji) enables each agent to detect whether change is local or network-wide. Lyapunov-derived ΔW=−α·δ_i·∇_W h_i suppresses both temporal and spatial error growth. Pairwise CBF h_ij≥0 is forward-invariant throughout adaptation with provably improving bounds.

LIVE SIMULATION — graph topology · message passing · shared GNN layers · actor-critic HJB · pairwise CBF · closed-loop · UUB convergence

Safety-Critical Control
Cyber-Physical Safety-Critical Systems

Safety assurance in RL-based control of constrained nonlinear systems using Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Time-Varying Barrier Lyapunov Functions (TVBLF). These embed safety constraints directly into optimal control synthesis, ensuring forward invariance h(x)≥0 throughout online adaptation. Actor-critic ADP provides the optimal policy while barrier functions guarantee constraint satisfaction. Applications: mobile robot formation, UAV tracking, output-feedback strict-feedback systems.

LIVE SIMULATION — CBF/TVBLF safety barriers · actor-critic RL · constrained tracking · barrier gradient · weight convergence

Lifelong Learning
Adaptive Continual Learning Control

Lifelong/continual deep RL frameworks for multi-task tracking control of uncertain nonlinear systems. Bellman-error-driven weight updates in multilayer NNs prevent catastrophic forgetting while adapting to new tasks. The critic NN approximates the value function, the actor NN generates optimal control, and an NN identifier estimates unknown dynamics — all updated online with Lyapunov-proven UUB stability. Covers state-feedback and SVD-based output-feedback settings.

LIVE SIMULATION — lifelong RL · actor-critic ADP · multi-task tracking · Bellman error update · no catastrophic forgetting · UUB
