About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Mechanical & Electrical Engineering Department at Wilkes University, Wilkes-Barre, PA. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Missouri University of Science and Technology (MS&T) in 2025, advised by Prof. Sarangapani Jagannathan.

My research centers on designing intelligent, safe, and adaptive control systems for uncertain nonlinear and cyber-physical systems — spanning adaptive optimal control, deep continual reinforcement learning, neuromorphic/spiking neural networks, and human–robot teaming.

My work has been funded by the Army Research Office (ARO), Office of Naval Research (ONR), and Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR). I am currently PI on pending NSF proposals in graph neural control and spiking neural networks.

I received my M.Tech. in Cyber-Physical Systems from IIT Jodhpur, India, working on integral sliding mode control for microgrid ripple mitigation funded by DST, India.

Neuromorphic Computing
Adaptive Spiking Neural Control

Safety-critical control using spiking neural networks (SNNs) — biologically inspired, event-driven architectures achieving 80% energy savings over conventional ANNs. The framework embeds safety via Augmented Barrier States (ABS) and uses SVD-regularized online weight update laws to handle spike discontinuities. A second thrust uses SNN-based intent observers for human–multi-robot collaboration with cooperative game-theoretic optimal control.

Human–Robot Teaming
Human–Robot Interaction & Teaming

A two-level adaptive control architecture for safe cooperative human–robot manipulation. The high-level controller uses an admittance model M̂ẍ+B̂ẋ+K̂x=F_h whose parameters are estimated online by an intent neural network. The generated reference trajectory feeds a distributed actor-critic RL controller (one per agent) solving coupled HJB equations with neighbor information. Safety is enforced via Barrier Lyapunov Functions + KKT conditions, projecting unsafe policies to safety-aware ones. All weights (critic, actor, intent NN) updated online simultaneously.

LIVE SIMULATION — Human force → admittance intent NN → actor-critic RL → BLF/KKT safety → plant · all weights online

Multi-Agent Systems
Adaptive Graph Neural Control

New foundations connecting non-stationary reinforcement learning, Lyapunov-based adaptive optimal control, and graph neural network function approximation. By sharing messages m_ji=φ_m(h_j,h_i,e_ji) along the network, each subsystem detects whether change originated locally or from a distant neighbor. Node updates h_i^(ℓ+1)=σ(W^(ℓ)·Σm_ji) use shared weights W^(ℓ) across all agents. Lyapunov-derived update laws ΔW=−α·δ_i·∇_W h_i suppress both temporal and spatial error growth. Pairwise CBF safety h_ij≥0 is forward-invariant throughout adaptation.

LIVE SIMULATION — Graph topology · message passing · shared GNN layers · actor-critic HJB · pairwise CBF · closed-loop · convergence

Safety-Critical Control
Cyber-Physical Safety-Critical Systems

Safety assurance in RL-based control of constrained nonlinear systems using Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Time-Varying Barrier Lyapunov Functions (TVBLF). These frameworks embed safety constraints directly into optimal control synthesis, ensuring forward invariance of safe sets throughout online adaptation. Applications: mobile robot formation control, UAV tracking, and output feedback control of strict-feedback systems.

Lifelong Learning
Adaptive Continual Learning Control

Lifelong/continual deep RL frameworks for multi-task tracking control of uncertain nonlinear systems. Online Bellman-error-driven weight update laws in multilayer NNs prevent catastrophic forgetting while adapting to new tasks. Covers state-feedback and output-feedback (SVD-based observer) settings for strict-feedback and control-affine system classes. Applications: n-link robot manipulators, mobile robots, UAVs, unmanned surface vehicles.
