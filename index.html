About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Mechanical & Electrical Engineering Department at Wilkes University, Wilkes-Barre, PA. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Missouri University of Science and Technology (MS&T) in 2025, advised by Prof. Sarangapani Jagannathan.

My research centers on designing intelligent, safe, and adaptive control systems for uncertain nonlinear and cyber-physical systems — spanning adaptive optimal control, deep continual reinforcement learning, neuromorphic/spiking neural networks, and human–robot teaming.

My work has been funded by the Army Research Office (ARO), Office of Naval Research (ONR), and Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR). I am currently PI on pending NSF proposals in graph neural control and spiking neural networks.

I received my M.Tech. in Cyber-Physical Systems from IIT Jodhpur, India, where I worked on integral sliding mode control for microgrid ripple mitigation funded by DST, India.

Neuromorphic Computing
Adaptive Spiking Neural Control

This research develops safety-critical control frameworks using spiking neural networks (SNNs) — biologically inspired, event-driven architectures that achieve up to 80% energy savings over conventional ANNs while maintaining formal safety guarantees. The framework embeds safety constraints directly within closed-loop dynamics via Augmented Barrier States (ABS), and uses SVD-regularized online weight update laws to overcome the instability caused by spike discontinuities. A second thrust addresses human–multi-robot collaboration with SNN-based intent observers enabling real-time trajectory estimation through sparse spike processing, combined with cooperative game-theoretic optimal control achieving 60% energy reduction in operation.

Human–Robot Teaming
Human–Robot Interaction

This research develops online learning frameworks for safe, adaptive, and cooperative human–robot teaming. Using admittance-based controllers, online intent estimation, and deep reinforcement learning, these systems enable robots to interpret human intent in real time and coordinate cooperative manipulation tasks with formal safety guarantees. Work spans single-robot HRI with force-based interaction to human-swarm coordination in complex multi-environment scenarios.

Multi-Agent Systems
Adaptive Graph Neural Control

This research creates new foundations connecting non-stationary reinforcement learning, Lyapunov-based adaptive optimal control, and graph neural network function approximation. By sharing messages along the network, each subsystem can detect whether a change originated locally or arrived from a distant neighbor and adapts its own learning rate accordingly — producing the first performance bound in cooperative control that provably improves as the network stabilizes. Lyapunov-derived update laws explicitly suppress both temporal and spatial approximation error growth pathways, keeping all weight errors bounded within a computable shrinking set. Continuous neighbor monitoring enables preemptive adaptation before disturbances arrive locally, with provably lower cumulative error than any reactive strategy. Forward invariance of pairwise safety constraints is certified throughout adaptation, with required clearances computed from actor weight error bounds.

Safety-Critical Control
Cyber-Physical Safety-Critical Systems

This research addresses safety assurance in reinforcement learning-based control of constrained nonlinear systems using Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Time-Varying Barrier Lyapunov Functions (TVBLF). The developed frameworks embed safety constraints directly into the optimal control synthesis, ensuring forward invariance of safe sets throughout online adaptation. Applications span mobile robot formation control, UAV tracking, and output feedback control of strict-feedback systems under state constraints.

Lifelong Learning
Adaptive Continual Learning Control

This research develops lifelong/continual deep reinforcement learning frameworks for multi-task tracking control of uncertain nonlinear systems. By incorporating online Bellman-error-driven weight update laws into multilayer neural networks, the methods prevent catastrophic forgetting while adapting to new environments and tasks. Applications span n-link robot manipulators, mobile robots, UAVs, and unmanned surface vehicles. The work covers both state-feedback and output-feedback (using SVD-based multilayer neural network observers) settings, and includes both strict-feedback and control-affine system classes.
